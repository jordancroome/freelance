Outcomes, not just a logo file

A useful identity reduces decision fatigue: your team knows which type, colour, and layout to use, and suppliers stop improvising. Projects are structured so strategy leads visuals — then the system scales to your website, packaging, signage, and campaigns.

Typical deliverables

  • Logo and wordmark, lockups, and clear-space rules
  • Colour palette and typography system
  • Core templates (e.g. stationery, social, pitch)
  • Guidelines you can hand to designers or printers

FAQ

Common questions.

What’s included in a branding project?

Typically: discovery, positioning notes, logo and core identity, colour and type rules, and practical guidelines so your team or suppliers apply the brand consistently. Collateral can be bundled or phased.

Do I need a full rebrand or just a logo?

It depends on consistency, ambition, and where the brand breaks down. Read the guide on logo vs full identity — most SMBs need more than a mark but less than a museum rebrand.

Can you work with our existing logo?

Yes, if the strategy still fits. I often run refresh projects: refine the mark, tighten typography, expand the system, and bring touchpoints up to standard.
