Logo design vs full brand identity — what do you actually need?

For founders and marketing leads who want clarity before they commission work.

Jordan Croome — brand, graphic, and product design. Cairns, Melbourne, and Australia-wide.

What a logo project is

A focused engagement to settle the mark (and often a simple colour/type pairing) so you can ship something credible quickly. Best when touchpoints are few, the audience is narrow, or you are validating an offer before investing in a system.

What a brand identity system adds

Beyond the logo: rules for typography, colour usage, imagery, voice cues, and core templates. The value is consistency at scale — fewer one-off decisions, cleaner handoffs to printers, web partners, or internal staff.

How to choose

  • Logo-first can work if you will not multiply channels in the next 12–18 months, or you accept a second-phase system later.
  • System-first pays off if you already juggle social, print, signage, pitch decks, or a digital product — inconsistency is costing trust.
  • Refresh vs full rebrand if an old mark has equity but everything around it is dated — often a lighter project than starting from zero.

Cairns SMBs

Tourism, retail, trades, and professional services often hit the wall when DIY Canva stacks stop matching the real quality of the operation. If that is you, see graphic design in Cairns for ongoing collateral after identity is set.
